Table of Contents

Namespace CalloraVoipSdk.WebRtc

Classes

RemoteTrack

A remote media track surfaced by TrackReceived (the W3C per-track model). Encoded frames arrive on FrameReceived.

WebRtcClient

The WebRTC peer facade (Level 1). Builds signalling-neutral IPeerConnections that run ICE, DTLS-SRTP, BUNDLE and RTP/RTCP internally; the app owns signalling and the codec (transport only). Zero-config new WebRtcClient() offers Opus over an ephemeral loopback endpoint with a fresh per-peer DTLS identity. See ADR-012 for the four-level design and the manager/DI tiers to come.

WebRtcConfiguration

Immutable configuration for a WebRtcClient (the direct-construction surface; the DI/options path adds a mutable counterpart in a later slice). All fields are optional — a zero-config new WebRtcClient() binds an ephemeral loopback endpoint, offers Opus audio, and uses a fresh per-peer DTLS identity.

WebRtcConnectException

Thrown by ConnectAsync(IPeerConnection, IWebRtcSignaling, WebRtcRole, CancellationToken) when the peer connection fails to establish — the connection reached Failed or was closed during negotiation (ICE or DTLS-SRTP did not complete).

WebRtcMediaStreamStats

A per-media-stream quality snapshot within a peer connection's WebRtcStats (the SDK's getStats per-stream breakdown). One entry per audio/video track (folded by MID), plus one per remote inbound source whose payload type could not be attributed to a track. The two directions carry different metrics — RoundTripTimeMs and PacketLoss describe our outbound stream (keyed per our sending SSRC, RFC 3550 §6.4.1), while JitterMs is our local receive-side jitter for the remote inbound source (RFC 3550 §A.8). A metric reads null until it is available for that stream — never a fabricated zero.

WebRtcOptions

Host-facing, mutable options for the WebRTC facade, consumed by AddCalloraWebRtc(...) — the dependency-injection counterpart to the immutable WebRtcConfiguration (mirrors how VoipOptions backs VoipConfiguration for the SIP facade).

WebRtcPeerConnectionExtensions

The signalling happy path (Level 1) for IPeerConnection: given an app-owned IWebRtcSignaling channel, the SDK drives the full offer/answer handshake and returns once the connection is established — the WebRTC counterpart to the SIP DialAndWaitUntilConnectedAsync. The neutral primitives (CreateOffer(), SetRemoteDescriptionAsync(string, CancellationToken), StartAsync(CancellationToken)) remain for callers that drive signalling themselves.

WebRtcRecorder

The built-in WebRTC recorder (L3 module). Attaches a media tap to a peer and streams every encoded frame (audio and video, both directions) into an IEncodedMediaSink. Usable standalone (new WebRtcRecorder().Start(peer, sink)) or registered as an IWebRtcClientModule and resolved via client.Modules.Get<IWebRtcRecorder>(). Transport-only: it captures the raw codec bitstream — turning it into a playable file is the sink's job.

WebRtcStats

A point-in-time statistics snapshot for a peer connection (the SDK's getStats). Transport counters, derived bitrates and the ICE state/selected pair are populated today; quality, video and congestion-control metrics are added in later slices and read null until then — a null value means "not yet measured", never a fabricated zero.

Structs

DtmfTone

One fully received inbound DTMF tone (RFC 4733 telephone-event), surfaced on DtmfReceived once per tone after its end-of-event packet.

EncodedFrame

One encoded media frame received on a RemoteTrack. Transport-only: Payload is the raw depacketised codec bitstream — the app owns decoding.

RecordedFrame

One encoded media frame captured for recording, tagged with its track kind and direction. Transport-only: Payload is the raw depacketised codec bitstream (the recorder does not decode).

Interfaces

IEncodedMediaSink

A destination for recorded encoded media. The recorder writes every captured RecordedFrame here (both directions, audio and video); the sink decides how to persist it — a per-track file, a container, an upload, an in-memory buffer, … Transport-only: payloads are the raw codec bitstream (no decoding). A container writer (Opus→Ogg, VP8→IVF, muxed MP4/…) is a concrete sink implementation.

IMediaTap

An L3 observer of the encoded media flowing through an IPeerConnection in both directions — the extension seam for recording, analytics, or feeding an AI pipeline without owning the peer. Attach with AttachMediaTap(IMediaTap) and dispose the handle to detach.

IPeerConnection

A signalling-neutral WebRTC peer connection: the app owns the signalling channel (WebSocket/HTTP/ Callora) and exchanges SDP through it, while this peer runs ICE, DTLS-SRTP, BUNDLE and the RTP/RTCP transport. Encoded media flows through SendAudioAsync(ReadOnlyMemory<byte>, CancellationToken)/SendVideoFrameAsync(ReadOnlyMemory<byte>, uint, CancellationToken) — the SDK is transport-only, so the app owns the codec. The central per-connection abstraction of the WebRTC facade, mirroring the SIP ICall.

IPeerConnectionManager

The L2 registry of peer connections opened through a IWebRtcClient — the WebRTC counterpart to the SIP ICallManager. Peers are tracked on creation and removed when disposed, so a multi-peer app (e.g. a conference) can enumerate its live connections without keeping its own list.

IWebRtcClient

The WebRTC peer facade — the happy-path entry point for building signalling-neutral peer connections. Mirrors the SIP IVoipClient; the advanced peer-registry manager tier is added in a later slice (ADR-012).

IWebRtcClientModule

Contract every WebRTC facade module implements to be discoverable through IWebRtcModuleRegistry — the L3 plugin seam, mirroring the SIP IVoipClientModule. Module packages define their own feature interfaces on top of this marker; the facade never references concrete module types.

IWebRtcModuleRegistry

Thread-safe registry resolving optional WebRTC facade modules by their feature contract.

IWebRtcRecorder

Records the encoded media flowing through a peer connection into an IEncodedMediaSink — an L3 module built on AttachMediaTap(IMediaTap). Transport-only: it captures the raw codec bitstream per track and direction; muxing to a playable container is the sink's job.

IWebRtcRecording

A running recording of a peer connection. Stop it with StopAsync(CancellationToken) (or by disposing) to detach from the peer and flush the sink.

IWebRtcSignaling

The app-owned signalling channel the SDK drives during ConnectAsync(IPeerConnection, IWebRtcSignaling, WebRtcRole, CancellationToken): a bidirectional transport (WebSocket, HTTP long-poll, Callora signalling, …) that carries SDP descriptions between the two peers. The SDK stays signalling-neutral — it only sends and receives SDP through this contract; the app decides how the bytes travel.

IWebRtcTrickleSignaling

Extends IWebRtcSignaling with out-of-band ICE candidate trickle (RFC 8838): alongside the offer/answer, the SDK sends each locally gathered candidate as it is discovered and applies remote candidates as they arrive, rather than relying only on the candidates the SDP carried. When an app supplies this on ConnectAsync(IPeerConnection, IWebRtcSignaling, WebRtcRole, CancellationToken), the SDK gathers server-reflexive candidates and runs the trickle exchange; a plain IWebRtcSignaling stays SDP-only. Candidate strings are RFC 8829 candidate:… lines — the wire form browsers exchange verbatim, so no conversion is needed at the app boundary.

Enums

MediaDirection

The direction of media observed by an IMediaTap.

PeerConnectionState

Lifecycle state of a WebRTC IPeerConnection, modelled on the W3C RTCPeerConnectionState (RFC 8829): a signalling-neutral view of where the peer is between creation and teardown. ICE consent and the DTLS handshake drive the transitions.

TrackKind

The media kind of a remote WebRTC track surfaced by TrackReceived.

WebRtcRole

The negotiation role a peer takes in the SDK-driven handshake (ConnectAsync(IPeerConnection, IWebRtcSignaling, WebRtcRole, CancellationToken)).
